What we do

ATD Research and Consultancy LTD are experts in gap analysis, service evaluation, service re-design and best practice recommendations. We support our clients to review their existing provisions, highlight what’s working and where improvements need to be made. We provide objective, independent and informed approaches to deliver the best outcomes for providers and service-users. We can qualitatively evaluate the impact of new services or make suggestions about where new services may need to be considered or implemented.

Our expertise is in consulting service-users and those most affected by health, justice and social issues; translating findings into pragmatic recommendations for service delivery, practice and policy. We provide cost-effective, evidence based ideas to support identified cohorts with a high social return on investment.

ATD Research and Consultancy LTD also provide bespoke training packages to services and providers wishing to engage in best practice and support services in bid writing for future opportunities.

We also offer youth mentoring services – please enquire for further information.

Training

ATD Research and Consultancy LTD provide specialist training on motivating substance misusing offenders based on extensive research and work with this cohort. The training advances the use of traditional recovery models and tools by encouraging consideration of quality of motivation, rather than its mere presence or absence. It considers how psychological frameworks can create spaces and environments that allow intrinsic motivation to flourish.

Other areas of training are available upon request e.g. using forensic psychology to inform therapeutic environments, working with female offenders, mental health awareness training. Please let us know your staff needs and we will be more than happy to discuss a bespoke package with you.

Previous Projects

ATD Research and Consultancy LTD specialises in the domains of Criminal Justice, Health and Well-Being with research and practitioner experience across prisons, probation, 3rd sector and local authority provisions. We have experience of devising and designing gender responsive services, working with substance misusing offenders as well as individuals with complex needs. Previous work includes evaluating substance misuse services in the community and prison service, evaluating provisions for offenders with complex needs including vulnerable adults and older offenders, evaluating substance misuse services for young people, securing grant funding for new services, formulating and fostering innovative partnership working arrangements, consulting on Department of Health initiatives.

Biography

Dr Sarah Senker PhD CPsychol is an award winning researcher and Chartered Psychologist with a specialism in Criminal Justice. She holds a PhD in Health Studies which focused on the recovery of offenders with a history of crack and heroin use. She has delivered one to one interventions and group work in custody and community settings with male and female offenders, including facilitating a recovery group in a local category B prison. She has worked as a National Criminal Justice Development Manager for a National mental health organisation, initiating and designing services for offenders. This work focused on the promotion of effective partnerships to ensure continuity of care and seamless provisions between health, social care and criminal justice with a particular emphasis on individuals with multiple and complex needs. Sarah is currently the Director of ATD Research and Consultancy providing training and consultancy services to providers wishing to evaluate services for offenders and make recommendations for best practice. Sarah’s passion is to use theory and research to inform practice and service delivery with a view to reduce re-offending and enhance well-being.
